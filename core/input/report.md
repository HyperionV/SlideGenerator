# Slide 1 — Industry Tailwinds

**Slide Header**: Vietnam Cosmetics Market: Favorable Demographics and Accelerating Digital Adoption
**Tagline**: Rapidly expanding middle class, high digital penetration, and a shift towards local authenticity drive robust 5.5% CAGR growth through 2030.

**Structural Demand Drivers Fueling Premiumization**

- The Vietnam cosmetics market is projected to reach $2.3B–$3.2B by 2030, reflecting a resilient average Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) of 5.5%.
- The middle class is projected to reach 26% of the population by 2026, with per capita income expected to reach $7,500 by 2030, enabling a sustained shift from necessity-based to discretionary purchases.
- Vietnamese consumers exhibit a high willingness to pay for quality, spending an average of $19–$21 monthly on makeup and skincare products, supporting a growth in the premium segment (25% of the market).

**Critical Channel Shift to Digital and Social Commerce**

- E-commerce is rapidly reshaping distribution, expected to account for 24% of total beauty sales by 2027 (up from 20.2% in 2024), actively capturing share from traditional offline channels.
- E-commerce platforms generated approximately $1.5B in beauty revenue in 2023, growing over 50% YoY, making platforms like Shopee and TikTok Shop critical control points for market access and consumer engagement.
- High smartphone penetration (over 75% of the population) facilitates rapid adoption of digital marketing and influencer-led sales models (KOLs/KOCs).

**High-Growth Niches and Functional Product Segments**

- Skincare dominates the product mix, holding the largest share at 43%, and is projected to reach $2.35B by 2032 (11.8% CAGR), driven by demand for advanced functional products (e.g., anti-aging, UV protection).
- The local natural/vegan segment is a high-growth niche, expanding at a 30% CAGR, driven by increasing consumer health consciousness and willingness to pay more for ethically sourced products.
- The men’s grooming segment, though small (1.3% of the total market), is experiencing rapid expansion with an 18% YoY growth rate, opening a new, underserved Total Addressable Market (TAM).

**Regulatory Support for Quality Local Players**

- The evolving regulatory environment, including new decrees (Circular 34/2025/TT-BYT) and enhanced enforcement of ASEAN CGMP standards, increases the cost of compliance for non-legitimate products.
- This structural change creates a competitive moat for compliant, scaled local brands by reducing the proliferation of counterfeit goods, thereby protecting brand equity and consumer trust.

### Key Drivers

#### 1. Rising Middle Class and Disposable Income

- **Mechanism**: Increased financial capacity allows consumers to shift from necessity-based spending to discretionary purchases, prioritizing beauty and personal care [133](https://www.marketsandata.com/industry-reports/vietnam-cosmetics-market)[67](https://www.imarcgroup.com/vietnam-cosmetics-market).
- **Tailwind/Horizon**: Tailwind / Long-term (>36m). Per capita income is projected to reach $7,500 by 2030 [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html), supporting sustained growth in premium segment purchasing.
- **Evidence**: Vietnam's middle class is projected to reach 26% of the population by 2026 [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html). The top 20% of earners contributed 45.39% of the country’s total income spent on cosmetics in 2022 [77](https://vietnam.incorp.asia/vietnam-cosmetics-market/).
- **Indicators to Watch**: GDP per capita growth, expansion of luxury brand presence (e.g., Estée Lauder boutique openings [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html)[76](https://www.techsciresearch.com/report/vietnam-cosmetics-market/8175.html)), and volume growth in the 25% premium segment [77](https://vietnam.incorp.asia/vietnam-cosmetics-market/).

K-Beauty remains a dominant source of imports, holding the largest foreign market share [71](https://www.trade.gov/market-intelligence/vietnam-beauty-and-personal-care-products)[118](https://export.gov.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/lifestyle-Vietnam-cosmetics-market-Overview.pdf).

### Structural Trends

#### 1. Digitalization and Omnichannel Shift in Retail

- **Trend Name**: Digitalization and Omnichannel Shift
- **So What**: This trend reduces geographical barriers to entry for international brands and compresses the time-to-market. It shifts competitive advantage from physical footprint to digital marketing expertise (SEO, social commerce, livestreaming), impacting unit economics by potentially lowering CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost) via high-conversion social channels [172](https://blog.applabx.com/top-5-digital-marketing-trends-driving-investment-opportunities-in-vietnam-2025-2030/)[74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf).
- **Evidence**: Offline retail market share dropped from 92% (2018) to 81.2% (2023) [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/). TikTok Shop now holds 18% of social commerce sales [172](https://blog.applabx.com/top-5-digital-marketing-trends-driving-investment-opportunities-in-vietnam-2025-2030/). Brands are rapidly expanding their presence on Shopee, Lazada, and TikTok [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/).

#### 2. Premiumization and Focus on Functional Skincare

- **Trend Name**: Premiumization and Focus on Functional Skincare
- **So What**: This raises the Average Selling Price (ASP) and gross margins for brands that successfully position themselves on quality, efficacy, and specialized functions (e.g., anti-aging, UV protection, ingredient-based science) [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html)[76](https://www.techsciresearch.com/report/vietnam-cosmetics-market/8175.html). It increases barriers to entry for low-end, generic products and favors brands with strong R&D or credible clinical backing.
- **Evidence**: The premium cosmetics segment accounts for 25% of the market and is growing significantly [77](https://vietnam.incorp.asia/vietnam-cosmetics-market/)[165](https://ocm-vn.com/en/thi-truong-my-pham-viet-nam-2023/). Skincare is the dominating segment, driven by demand for specific treatments for acne, pigmentation, and aging [76](https://www.techsciresearch.com/report/vietnam-cosmetics-market/8175.html).

#### 3. Regulatory Modernization and Quality Standards Enforcement

- **Trend Name**: Regulatory Modernization and Quality Standards Enforcement
- **So What**: Upcoming decrees will enhance pre-market controls and post-market surveillance, requiring tighter compliance with ASEAN CGMP standards [77](https://vietnam.incorp.asia/vietnam-cosmetics-market/). This increases the cost of compliance for domestic and foreign manufacturers but provides a competitive moat for legitimate players by reducing the proliferation of counterfeit goods, thereby protecting brand equity and ROIC (Return on Invested Capital) [77](https://vietnam.incorp.asia/vietnam-cosmetics-market/)[120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/).
- **Evidence**: The Drug Administration Department is drafting a comprehensive decree (expected 2025) to align Vietnam’s regulations with the ASEAN Cosmetics Regulatory Scheme [77](https://vietnam.incorp.asia/vietnam-cosmetics-market/). A nationwide campaign to crack down on counterfeit goods was launched in May 2025 [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/).

#### 4. Rise of the Male Grooming Consumer

- **Trend Name**: Mainstreaming of Male Grooming
- **So What**: This opens a new, high-growth segment (1.3% of the total market [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html)) previously underserved. It expands the total addressable market (TAM) and offers brands opportunities to diversify revenue streams. Competition is lower than in the saturated female segment, potentially yielding higher initial market share capture and brand loyalty [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html)[67](https://www.imarcgroup.com/vietnam-cosmetics-market).
- **Evidence**: The male grooming segment is experiencing rapid expansion with an 18% YoY growth rate [67](https://www.imarcgroup.com/vietnam-cosmetics-market). The men’s cosmetics market was valued at $30M in 2022 [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html).

## Value Chain Analysis

### Executive Summary

The Vietnamese Cosmetics Value Chain is characterized by extreme dependency on foreign brand ownership and a rapid shift of profit concentration toward downstream digital distribution channels. The market size is projected to grow from **$1.7B in 2024** to **$2.8B by 2032**, driven by rising disposable incomes (GDP per capita CAGR 6.10% 2018-2028 [178](https://ijaem.net/issue_dcp/Vietnam%20s%20Cosmetics%20Industry%20Current%20Situation,%20Opportunities,%20and%20Challenges%20for%20Domestic%20Producers%20in%20the%20New%20Context.pdf)) and a high consumer preference for imported products [174](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/doing-business-guide/vietnam/sector-insights/vietnam-s-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-for-growing-market)[182](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnams-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-growing-market.html/). Foreign players control **90-93%** of the market [174](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/doing-business-guide/vietnam/sector-insights/vietnam-s-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-for-growing-market)[182](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnams-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-growing-market.html/). Bargaining power is highly concentrated in **Foreign Brand Owners** (IP/R&D) and **E-commerce Platforms** (access/data). Strategic opportunities lie in acquiring established specialty retailers or investing in localized, sustainably branded manufacturing (e.g., natural/vegan segments) that leverage Vietnam’s abundant local ingredients.

### Strategic Implications

#### 1. Shift of Control Points to Digital Platforms and Data

The competitive battleground is rapidly shifting from physical shelf space to digital screen space. E-commerce channels are gaining traction at a high CAGR (inferred >20% [185](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/retail-industry-in-vietnam)[74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf)), making platforms like Shopee and TikTok Shop critical control points [138](https://the-shiv.com/vietnams-cosmetics-industry/)[74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf).

- **Implication**: Foreign entrants can bypass traditional distribution costs through social commerce (TikTok Shop) [138](https://the-shiv.com/vietnams-cosmetics-industry/). Investors should favor companies with strong digital marketing capabilities and proprietary customer data, as this capability will determine future market share and margin control.
- **Evidence**: La Roche-Posay was the leading cosmetics brand by e-commerce revenue in 2023 [184](https://www.statista.com/statistics/1480894/vietnam-market-share-of-cosmetics-brands-based-on-e-commerce-revenue/), demonstrating the success of brands that master online channels and influencer marketing (KOLs/KOCs) [74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf).

#### 2. Localized Niche as a Defensible Position

While foreign brands dominate the mass and premium segments, Vietnam’s advantage in natural/herbal ingredients offers a defensible niche for local players focusing on sustainability and vegan products [178](https://ijaem.net/issue_dcp/Vietnam%20s%20Cosmetics%20Industry%20Current%20Situation,%20Opportunities,%20and%20Challenges%20for%20Domestic%20Producers%20in%20the%20New%20Context.pdf)[74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf).

- **Implication**: Investment in local brands that secure vegan certifications and leverage local sourcing (coconut, green tea, aloe vera) [178](https://ijaem.net/issue_dcp/Vietnam%20s%20Cosmetics%20Industry%20Current%20Situation,%20Opportunities,%20and%20Challenges%20for%20Domestic%20Producers%20in%20the%20New%20Context.pdf)[182](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnams-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-growing-market.html/) can capture the growing environmentally conscious consumer segment [138](https://the-shiv.com/vietnams-cosmetics-industry/). This strategy allows local brands to compete on value and ethics rather than just price, moving them out of the commoditized manufacturing segment.
- **Evidence**: Cocoon is the only Vietnamese brand in the top 10 online sellers [74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf), successfully leveraging a **100% vegan, cruelty-free** identity and targeting a niche demographic [74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf).

#### 3. Investment in Downstream Consolidation (Specialty Retail)

The fragmented nature of traditional retail (mom-and-pop stores still hold 52.26% of overall retail market size [185](https://www.mordorintelligence.com/industry-reports/retail-industry-in-vietnam)) contrasts sharply with the consolidating modern specialty trade (Watsons, Guardian) [174](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/doing-business-guide/vietnam/sector-insights/vietnam-s-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-for-growing-market).

- **Implication**: Specialty cosmetic retailers that offer in-store product testing and beauty consultations (critical for a market where consumers want to experience products [187](https://en.vietstock.vn/2024/03/e-commerce-yet-to-replace-traditional-tradechannels-38-548559.htm)) while integrating strong online channels (Omni-channel) present attractive M&A targets. They control the last mile of consumer trust and experience.
- **Evidence**: Traditional trade channels continue to be sustainable, and offline sales often increase dramatically when brands expand distribution, confirming the need for an integrated Omni-channel strategy [187](https://en.vietstock.vn/2024/03/e-commerce-yet-to-replace-traditional-tradechannels-38-548559.htm).

#### 4. Regulatory and Import Risk Mitigation

High reliance on imports exposes the value chain to regulatory changes, specifically concerning product notification and compliance with bodies like the Drug Administration of Vietnam (DAV) [182](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnams-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-growing-market.html/).

- **Implication**: Foreign companies must secure strong local distribution partnerships to navigate the regulatory landscape efficiently (e.g., obtaining the Letter of Authorization and managing the 7-10 day notification process) [182](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnams-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-growing-market.html/). Exemptions from the Certificate of Free Sale (CFS) for CPTPP and ASEAN members reduce logistical barriers for regional entrants [182](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnams-emerging-cosmetics-industry-strong-potential-growing-market.html/).

### Conclusion

The Vietnamese cosmetics value chain offers high-growth opportunities (6.7% CAGR [79](https://www.gmiresearch.com/report/vietnam-cosmetic-market/)) but requires strategic navigation around the dominance of foreign IP and the accelerating power of digital distribution platforms. Investment strategies should focus on **acquiring scale in specialty/online retail** (where EBITDA concentrates) or **funding niche local brands** (like Cocoon) that can establish a strong, defensible position based on sustainability and local ingredient sourcing, thereby challenging the commoditization of domestic manufacturing.

### Competitive Dynamics Synthesis

**Control Points and Bargaining Power:**
Bargaining power is fundamentally divided between **Brand Power** (held by the multinational and specialized international players due to perceived quality and trust [118](https://export.gov.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/lifestyle-Vietnam-cosmetics-market-Overview.pdf)) and **Channel Power** (increasingly held by E-commerce platforms and Health & Beauty Specialists [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/)).

- **Brand Power:** Multinationals sustain dominance through massive marketing budgets and established brand loyalty, mitigating the high cost of tariffs (up to 33%) [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html)[118](https://export.gov.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/lifestyle-Vietnam-cosmetics-market-Overview.pdf).
- **Channel Power:** E-commerce platforms like Shopee and TikTok Shop control discovery and price competition, commanding significant bargaining leverage over both local and international brands, forcing them to engage in live shopping and deep discounting [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/). The shift of beauty sales online (20.2% in 2024) [68](https://vietnamnet.vn/en/vn-cosmetics-market-local-producers-still-hold-modest-share-2256214.html) means retailers are now essential partners, capturing margin pools previously held by traditional distributors.

**Differentiation and Disruption:**
Competitive differentiation is increasingly focused on **Digital Engagement** and **Localization**.

- **Leading/Catching Up:** Incumbents (Unilever, L'Oréal) are leading on sheer scale but are catching up on digital agility (live streams, platform optimization) [131](https://www.scribd.com/document/542292466/Beauty-and-Personal-Care-in-Vietnam-Full-Market-Report). Specialized Internationals (K-Beauty) lead on trend-setting and product innovation [67](https://www.imarcgroup.com/vietnam-cosmetics-market).
- **Disrupting:** Local brands (Cocoon) are disrupting by leveraging authentic localization and the high-growth natural/organic niche [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html). E-commerce platforms are the primary disruptors of the traditional retail model [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/).

**Substitution Risks:**
Substitution risk is high in the mass market due to strong price sensitivity ($19–$21 average monthly spend) [118](https://export.gov.il/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/lifestyle-Vietnam-cosmetics-market-Overview.pdf) and the prevalence of counterfeit goods [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/). However, substitution risk is mitigated for premium/specialized brands that deliver on perceived quality and efficacy, particularly in the high-demand Skin Care segment.

**Investor Implications and M&A Thesis:**
The competitive dynamics suggest several strategic investment theses:

1.  **Digital-First Acquisition Thesis:** Target high-growth domestic brands (like Cocoon) that have proven success in the natural/organic niche ($7.84M revenue 2022) [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html) and demonstrated digital mastery. These firms offer localized product intelligence and high revenue growth rates (e.g., 1,315% for Cocoon 2020-2022) [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html), making them ideal M&A targets for foreign players seeking immediate market penetration and localization.
2.  **Channel Infrastructure Investment:** Invest in the rapid expansion of specialized physical retailers (like Hasaki or Guardian) which act as trusted, high-touch points for premium imported goods, mitigating counterfeit risk [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/). This supports the premiumization trend (expected to recover strongly) [131](https://www.scribd.com/document/542292466/Beauty-and-Personal-Care-in-Vietnam-Full-Market-Report).
3.  **Margin Protection in Digital:** Brands must invest heavily in data-driven marketing and influencer ROI tracking to ensure profitable growth on e-commerce platforms, which often demand promotional pricing [124](https://aggero.io/how-much-influencer-roi-can-brands-get/). ### Executive Summary

The Vietnamese Cosmetics and Personal Care market presents compelling M&A opportunities, driven by a projected market size of **US$2.74 billion by 2025** and a strong underlying consumer shift toward premium and natural products [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/). While Vietnam-specific transaction data is scarce, global beauty benchmarks indicate median M&A valuation multiples of approximately **13.7x EV/EBITDA** and **2.6x EV/Revenue** [154](https://www.capstonepartners.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Capstone-Partners-Beauty-MA-Coverage-Report-May-2023.pdf). M&A activity in Vietnam is increasingly focused on consolidation and value creation, moving away from high domestic megadeals, forcing acquirers to adopt rigorous, multi-dimensional due diligence, including deep scrutiny of ESG factors and complex regulatory compliance [140](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/vn/pdf/service_brochure/2024/11/m-and-a-vietnam-2024-en.pdf). Successful investment strategies must target high-growth local brands, leverage global operating expertise to realize post-acquisition synergies, and navigate the fragmented market structure [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/).

### Key Drivers for M&A

Structural forces are aligning to push both strategic and financial investors into the Vietnamese cosmetics sector, seeking scale and access to the burgeoning middle class.

- **Expanding Consumer Purchasing Power**: GDP per capita is projected to reach **US$4,580 in 2024** [140](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/vn/pdf/service_brochure/2024/11/m-and-a-vietnam-2024-en.pdf), contributing to a rapidly expanding middle class with increasing disposable income, which directly fuels demand for higher-margin premium and imported beauty products [140](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/vn/pdf/service_brochure/2024/11/m-and-a-vietnam-2024-en.pdf).
- **Demand for Specialized Product Offerings**: The market shows a growing preference for natural and organic beauty, with the segment reaching **$239 million in 2023** [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/). M&A is driven by the necessity to acquire local brands (e.g., Cocoon) that leverage indigenous ingredients and cater to this clean beauty trend [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/).
- **Strategic Consolidation for Distribution**: Foreign brands currently hold approximately **90% market share** [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/), making the market highly competitive. Acquisitions are utilized to secure established local distribution networks and omnichannel presence (e.g., Health & Beauty Specialists, e-commerce platforms like Shopee and TikTok Shop) [120](https://www.vietnam-briefing.com/news/vietnam-beauty-personal-care-sector-market-entry.html/).
- **Favorable Manufacturing & Supply Chain Dynamics**: Vietnam’s role as a regional production hub, supported by significant FDI flows (64% targeting manufacturing) [140](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/vn/pdf/service_brochure/2024/11/m-and-a-vietnam-2024-en.pdf), encourages strategic vertical integration and supply chain diversification, particularly through the "China+1" strategy [145](https://www.scribd.com/document/805301616/Vietnam-M-a-Report-2024).

### Key Considerations from Acquirer Perspective

Acquirers entering or expanding in Vietnam must apply heightened discipline and comprehensive analysis to mitigate risks associated with the evolving market and regulatory environment.

- **Rigorous, Multi-Dimensional Due Diligence (Financial and ESG)**: Acquirers must perform due diligence that extends beyond traditional financial and legal reviews to incorporate material ESG factors and commercial viability, which is critical for justifying valuations based on long-term sustainability and resilience [140](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/vn/pdf/service_brochure/2024/11/m-and-a-vietnam-2024-en.pdf).
- **Navigating Complex Regulatory and Competition Hurdles**: Transactions must adhere to strict merger control thresholds set by the Vietnam Competition Commission (VCC), which requires notification if the deal value exceeds **VND 1,000 billion** (approx. US$40 million) [160](https://www.viac.vn/images/Resources/Publications/Vietnam_Chapter_Quang-TruongNhat/iflr-ma-report-2021-vietnam.pdf). Furthermore, the VCC may scrutinize control factors beyond formal ownership structures [140](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/vn/pdf/service_brochure/2024/11/m-and-a-vietnam-2024-en.pdf).
- **Valuation Discipline Focused on Future Cash Flows**: Given the market volatility and a general decline in overall Vietnamese M&A multiples (average EV/EBITDA declined from 16.5x in 2021 to **11.5x in 2023** [145](https://www.scribd.com/document/805301616/Vietnam-M-a-Report-2024)[153](https://fiingroup.vn/upload/docs/sailing-through-challenges-unlocking-success-vietnam-ma-2024.pdf)), buyers must rely on forward-looking cash flow projections and carefully stress-test forecasts to confirm financial resilience and growth sustainability [140](https://assets.kpmg.com/content/dam/kpmg/vn/pdf/service_brochure/2024/11/m-and-a-vietnam-2024-en.pdf).

### 2. Business Model & Operations

#### 2.1 Revenue Model

- **Revenue Streams Breakdown:** Primarily derived from the sale of color cosmetics (makeup) and, more recently, skincare products (DA by M.O.I) [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)[60](https://www1.hkexnews.hk/app/sehk/2024/106846/a120827/sehk24100901149.pdf). The Baby Skin Cushion line is identified as the "cash cow" revenue driver [55](https://www.vietdata.vn/post/th%E1%BB%8B-ph%E1%BA%A7n-t%C4%83ng-v%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3t-b%E1%BA%ADc-1-dn-duy-nh%E1%BA%A5t-c%E1%BB%A7a-ng%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di-vi%E1%BB%87t-l%E1%BB%8Dt-top-10-ng%C3%A0nh-m%E1%BB%B9-ph%E1%BA%A9m-trang-%C4%91i%E1%BB%83m)[59](https://www.vietdata.vn/post/th%E1%BB%8B-ph%E1%BA%A7n-t%C4%83ng-v%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3t-b%E1%BA%ADc-1-dn-duy-nh%E1%BA%A5t-c%E1%BB%A7a-ng%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di-vi%E1%BB%87t-l%E1%BB%8Dt-top-10-ng%C3%A0nh-m%E1%BB%B9-ph%E1%BA%A9m-trang-%C4%91i%E1%BB%83m?srsltid=AfmBOor7aSWGPiZnGJHyKvf3yEeqj4ysJfCV9AN2Zl7609FMAyaI25Ab).
- **Pricing Strategy & Structure:** Mid-range segment pricing, aiming to be accessible to the Vietnamese middle class (22-30 age group, 5M to 10M VND income) [58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572)[61](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-bach-khoa-dai-hoc-quoc-gia-thanh-pho-ho-chi-minh/engineering-economics/moi-cosmetics-bds-dreams/65018128). The company accepts lower margins to ensure competitive pricing against international brands [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)[58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572). Example pricing: Limited edition lipstick at 379,000 VND [58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572).
- **Customer Segments:** Core target: Working women/office workers (22-30, middle class) [58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572). Secondary target: Younger demographic ("Trend girl," 16-21) [58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572). Over 90% of customers are female [61](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-bach-khoa-dai-hoc-quoc-gia-thanh-pho-ho-chi-minh/engineering-economics/moi-cosmetics-bds-dreams/65018128).
- **Sales Motion & Distribution Methods:** Utilizes a wide network of approximately 1,000 distribution agents nationwide [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)[64](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-a-vietnamese-beauty-brands-triumph-at-the-asia-pacific-business-awards.html). Products are also sold through major retail partners (Guardian, Hasaki, BeautyBox, MediCare) [11](https://moi-cosmetics.vn/blogs/news/he-thong-cua-hang-phan-phoi-m-o-i-cosmetics-7)[14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html) and e-commerce platforms (Shopee, Tiki, Lazada) [57](https://www.scribd.com/document/714770710/MOI-COSMETICS)[61](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-bach-khoa-dai-hoc-quoc-gia-thanh-pho-ho-chi-minh/engineering-economics/moi-cosmetics-bds-dreams/65018128).

#### 2.2 Operational Framework

- **Core Operational Processes:** Product design and concept development are managed internally in Vietnam, heavily influenced by the Creative Director [5](https://www.vietchallenge.org/post/m-o-i-cosmetics-and-the-dream-of-becoming-the-first-choice-of-every-vietnamese-woman). Manufacturing is outsourced entirely to leading factories in South Korea [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)[63](https://www.scribd.com/document/842228606/group-assignment-en).
- **Technology & Systems Infrastructure:** The company leverages digital platforms for sales (e-commerce) [57](https://www.scribd.com/document/714770710/MOI-COSMETICS) and social media for marketing, with over 400,000 followers as of 2024 [24](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-wins-the-corporate-excellence-award.html).
- **Supply Chain & Logistics:** Sourcing is concentrated in South Korea [63](https://www.scribd.com/document/842228606/group-assignment-en). Key suppliers include Cosmax Inc. and Yet Inc. [54](https://letsfinder.net/cong-ty-tnhh-m-o-i-cosmetics/)[62](https://www.trademo.com/companies/m-o-i-cosmetics-co-ltd/32736135). Products are certified in both Vietnam and Korea [61](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-bach-khoa-dai-hoc-quoc-gia-thanh-pho-ho-chi-minh/engineering-economics/moi-cosmetics-bds-dreams/65018128).
- **QA/Compliance Mechanisms:** Adherence to international quality standards via Korean manufacturing partners [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html). All products must secure a Cosmetic Product Notification (CPN) from the Drug Administration of Vietnam (DAV) [102](https://greennrj.com.vn/cosmetic-product-registration-imported-vietnam/).
- **Capacity, Throughput, Scalability:** Achieved a total sales volume of 4 million units by the end of 2022 [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html). Scalability is enabled by the outsourced Korean manufacturing model and the extensive 1,000-agent distribution network [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)[64](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-a-vietnamese-beauty-brands-triumph-at-the-asia-pacific-business-awards.html).

#### 2.3 Strategic Partnerships

- **Distribution Partners:** Major Vietnamese retail chains including Guardian, Hasaki, BeautyBox, MediCare, and AB Beauty World [11](https://moi-cosmetics.vn/blogs/news/he-thong-cua-hang-phan-phoi-m-o-i-cosmetics-7)[14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html).

### 5. Market Analysis

#### 5.1 Industry Context

- **Industry Definition:** Cosmetics and personal care products market in Vietnam [5](https://www.vietchallenge.org/post/m-o-i-cosmetics-and-the-dream-of-becoming-the-first-choice-of-every-vietnamese-woman).
- **Market Size & Growth:** Market size estimates for 2024 range from $1.7 billion [67](https://www.imarcgroup.com/vietnam-cosmetics-market) to $2.66 billion [68](https://vietnamnet.vn/en/vn-cosmetics-market-local-producers-still-hold-modest-share-2256214.html)[69](https://en.vietnamplus.vn/cosmetics-market-local-producers-still-hold-modest-share-post280393.vnp)[73](https://vietnamnews.vn/economy/1651348/cosmetics-market-local-producers-still-hold-a-modest-share.html)[74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf)[75](https://beautycarexpo.com/en/xu-huong-thi-truong-nganh-lam-dep-viet-nam-2024/).
- **Growth Outlook:** Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR) projected between 4.2% [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html) and 5.74% [76](https://www.techsciresearch.com/report/vietnam-cosmetics-market/8175.html) for the 2025–2030 forecast period.
- **Regulatory Environment:** Governed by the Drug Administration of Vietnam (DAV) under the Ministry of Health (MoH) [102](https://greennrj.com.vn/cosmetic-product-registration-imported-vietnam/). Requires a Cosmetic Product Notification (CPN) for all products [102](https://greennrj.com.vn/cosmetic-product-registration-imported-vietnam/). The regulatory environment is tightening with recent updates (Circular 34/2025/TT-BYT) [104](https://www.globalcosmeticsnews.com/vietnam-updates-cosmetic-notification-rules-with-digital-enhancements-and-clarified-procedures/) and forthcoming structural changes (Draft Decree expected July 1, 2026) [112](https://www.productcomplianceinstitute.com/it/2025/09/12/vietnam-draft-decree-on-the-management-of-cosmetics/)[115](https://globalcosing.chemradar.com/lawinfo/news/detail/eqvws7uhc8ow).

#### 5.2 Competitive Positioning

- **Direct & Adjacent Competitors:** Competes with established global players who dominate the market (e.g., L'Oréal, Unilever) [72](https://www.marknteladvisors.com/research-library/vietnam-cosmetics-market.html). M.O.I is positioned in the mid-range segment against brands like 3CE and Bonjours [58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572).
- **Market Share Estimates:** M.O.I is the only Vietnamese enterprise in the Top 10 makeup market share [55](https://www.vietdata.vn/post/th%E1%BB%8B-ph%E1%BA%A7n-t%C4%83ng-v%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3t-b%E1%BA%ADc-1-dn-duy-nh%E1%BA%A5t-c%E1%BB%A7a-ng%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di-vi%E1%BB%87t-l%E1%BB%8Dt-top-10-ng%C3%A0nh-m%E1%BB%B9-ph%E1%BA%A9m-trang-%C4%91i%E1%BB%83m)[70](https://cmg.asia/vietnamese-brand-m-o-i-cosmetics-founded-by-ho-ngoc-ha-rises-to-top-10-in-market-share-rankings-with-exponential-growth.html). Market share grew from 0.9% (2018) to 3.2% (2022) [70](https://cmg.asia/vietnamese-brand-m-o-i-cosmetics-founded-by-ho-ngoc-ha-rises-to-top-10-in-market-share-rankings-with-exponential-growth.html).
- **Relative Scale:** Foreign brands command approximately 90% of the Vietnamese market share [70](https://cmg.asia/vietnamese-brand-m-o-i-cosmetics-founded-by-ho-ngoc-ha-rises-to-top-10-in-market-share-rankings-with-exponential-growth.html)[71](https://www.trade.gov/market-intelligence/vietnam-beauty-and-personal-care-products).
- **Competitive Advantages:** Localization (products suited for Vietnamese conditions) [70](https://cmg.asia/vietnamese-brand-m-o-i-cosmetics-founded-by-ho-ngoc-ha-rises-to-top-10-in-market-share-rankings-with-exponential-growth.html), accessible pricing [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html), and a strong, celebrity-driven brand presence [55](https://www.vietdata.vn/post/th%E1%BB%8B-ph%E1%BA%A7n-t%C4%83ng-v%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3t-b%E1%BA%ADc-1-dn-duy-nh%E1%BA%A5t-c%E1%BB%A7a-ng%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di-vi%E1%BB%87t-l%E1%BB%8Dt-top-10-ng%C3%A0nh-m%E1%BB%B9-ph%E1%BA%A9m-trang-%C4%91i%E1%BB%83m).
- **Vulnerabilities:** Heavy reliance on celebrity co-founder [22](https://cmg.asia/moi-cosmetics-and-global-giant-disney.html); exposure to counterfeit products [77](https://vietnam.incorp.asia/vietnam-cosmetics-market/)[79](https://www.gmiresearch.com/report/vietnam-cosmetic-market/); potential channel risk due to reliance on a large network of 1,000 agents [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html).

#### 5.3 Customer Analysis

- **Target Demographics:** Core is 22-30 year-old working women (middle class) [58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572)[61](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-bach-khoa-dai-hoc-quoc-gia-thanh-pho-ho-chi-minh/engineering-economics/moi-cosmetics-bds-dreams/65018128). Over 90% female customer base [61](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-bach-khoa-dai-hoc-quoc-gia-thanh-pho-ho-chi-minh/engineering-economics/moi-cosmetics-bds-dreams/65018128).
- **Demand Drivers:** Consumers seek international quality, affordability, and are heavily influenced by Key Opinion Leaders (KOLs) and influencers [58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572)[74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf).
- **Purchasing Criteria:** Quality, affordable pricing, and clear product descriptions [58](https://www.studocu.vn/vn/document/truong-dai-hoc-fpt/consumer-behavior/group-3-moi-cosmetics-written-report/86827572). Most sales volume (80%) comes from products priced under 500,000 VND [74](https://sinhvien.dinhtienminh.net/wp-content/uploads/2024/09/Cosmetics-Market-Insight-in-Vietnam-E-commerce-2024-How-to-win.pdf).

### 6. Strategic Assessment

#### 6.1 Growth Opportunities

- **Market Expansion:** Targeting the capture of 90% of the market share currently held by international brands within the next five years [55](https://www.vietdata.vn/post/th%E1%BB%8B-ph%E1%BA%A7n-t%C4%83ng-v%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3t-b%E1%BA%ADc-1-dn-duy-nh%E1%BA%A5t-c%E1%BB%A7a-ng%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di-vi%E1%BB%87t-l%E1%BB%8Dt-top-10-ng%C3%A0nh-m%E1%BB%B9-ph%E1%BA%A9m-trang-%C4%91i%E1%BB%83m)[70](https://cmg.asia/vietnamese-brand-m-o-i-cosmetics-founded-by-ho-ngoc-ha-rises-to-top-10-in-market-share-rankings-with-exponential-growth.html).
- **Product Roadmap & Adjacencies:** Expansion beyond color cosmetics and skincare into haircare, intensive care (anti-aging), and mother/baby segments [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html). Development of vegan products to align with sustainability trends [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html).
- **Strategic Initiatives/Investments:** Planned Series B funding to support further market expansion and product line development [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)[55](https://www.vietdata.vn/post/th%E1%BB%8B-ph%E1%BA%A7n-t%C4%83ng-v%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3t-b%E1%BA%ADc-1-dn-duy-nh%E1%BA%A5t-c%E1%BB%A7a-ng%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di-vi%E1%BB%87t-l%E1%BB%8Dt-top-10-ng%C3%A0nh-m%E1%BB%B9-ph%E1%BA%A9m-trang-%C4%91i%E1%BB%83m). Focus on building customer loyalty to reduce Customer Acquisition Costs (CAC) [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html).
- **Scalability Analysis:** Scalability is supported by the established outsourced Korean manufacturing base and the robust, nationwide distribution network [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)[63](https://www.scribd.com/document/842228606/group-assignment-en).

#### 6.2 Risk Factors

- **Operational:** Management of the large, decentralized network of 1,000 agents [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html). Potential supply chain risk tied to single-country manufacturing source (Korea) [63](https://www.scribd.com/document/842228606/group-assignment-en).
- **Financial:** Need to verify the significant revenue increase implied by the US$12 million context figure [User Input]. Sensitivity to changes in celebrity endorsement effectiveness [22](https://cmg.asia/moi-cosmetics-and-global-giant-disney.html).
- **Market/Competitive:** Intense competition from foreign brands [70](https://cmg.asia/vietnamese-brand-m-o-i-cosmetics-founded-by-ho-ngoc-ha-rises-to-top-10-in-market-share-rankings-with-exponential-growth.html). Risk to brand integrity from counterfeit cosmetic products prevalent in the market [77](https://vietnam.incorp.asia/vietnam-cosmetics-market/)[79](https://www.gmiresearch.com/report/vietnam-cosmetic-market/).
- **Regulatory/Legal:** High compliance burden due to continuous regulatory updates (e.g., Circular 34/2025/TT-BYT) [104](https://www.globalcosmeticsnews.com/vietnam-updates-cosmetic-notification-rules-with-digital-enhancements-and-clarified-procedures/)[107](https://www.tilleke.com/insights/vietnam-updates-regulations-on-cosmetic-notification/34/). Potential penalties for failure to maintain the mandatory Product Information File (PIF) [106](https://azregistration.com/medical-device-regulatory-in-vietnam/cosmetic-products-notification-in-vietnam/). Risk of non-compliant advertising claims under the forthcoming Draft Decree [113](https://acsvlegal.com/media/file/1872/download?token=6de0e2ddd1894e46eae9ec37f72fdc66845c7e318dbd756e4d601a94c70d0af3&content-disposition=inline).
- **Key Person & Concentration Risks:** High dependency on the brand image and marketing power of co-founder Hồ Ngọc Hà [22](https://cmg.asia/moi-cosmetics-and-global-giant-disney.html)[55](https://www.vietdata.vn/post/th%E1%BB%8B-ph%E1%BA%A7n-t%C4%83ng-v%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3t-b%E1%BA%ADc-1-dn-duy-nh%E1%BA%A5t-c%E1%BB%A7a-ng%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di-vi%E1%BB%87t-l%E1%BB%8Dt-top-10-ng%C3%A0nh-m%E1%BB%B9-ph%E1%BA%A9m-trang-%C4%91i%E1%BB%83m).

#### 6.3 Competitive Moat

- **Sustainable Advantages:** **Localization** (products designed specifically for Vietnamese users, e.g., Baby Skin Cushion) [70](https://cmg.asia/vietnamese-brand-m-o-i-cosmetics-founded-by-ho-ngoc-ha-rises-to-top-10-in-market-share-rankings-with-exponential-growth.html); **Brand Equity** (strong, celebrity-backed brand recognition and trust [55](https://www.vietdata.vn/post/th%E1%BB%8B-ph%E1%BA%A7n-t%C4%83ng-v%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3t-b%E1%BA%ADc-1-dn-duy-nh%E1%BA%A5t-c%E1%BB%A7a-ng%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Di-vi%E1%BB%87t-l%E1%BB%8Dt-top-10-ng%C3%A0nh-m%E1%BB%B9-ph%E1%BA%A9m-trang-%C4%91i%E1%BB%83m)); **Distribution** (extensive 1,000-agent network and presence in major retail chains [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)).
- **Barriers to Entry:** High barrier for new domestic entrants to replicate the established network and achieve the perceived international quality standard [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html)[66](https://www.watsons.vn/en/blog/trang-diem/m-o-i-cosmetics-la-thuong-hieu-my-pham-cua-nuoc-nao-su-dung-co-tot-khong-top-5-san-pham-noi-bat/). Foreign brands face challenges in localizing products and matching M.O.I’s price point while maintaining quality [14](https://cmg.asia/m-o-i-cosmetics-journey-from-curiosity-to-4-million-beauty-products-sold.html).
- **Durability Assessment:** The moat's durability is high regarding market penetration and distribution but may be vulnerable to shifts in consumer preference away from celebrity endorsements or increased price competition from large multinationals [22](https://cmg.asia/moi-cosmetics-and-global-giant-disney.html).

## Insight Reasoning

**Rapidly Growing Domestic Beauty Champion in a High-Growth Market**
The company has achieved an average annual growth rate of 150% (excluding 2021) [35][40], positioning it to capture significant share in the expanding Vietnamese cosmetics market, which is projected to grow at a 5.5% Compound Annual Growth Rate through 2030 [72][76].

**Unrivaled Local Market Leadership Challenging Foreign Dominance**
M.O.I is the only Vietnamese enterprise consistently ranked in the Top 10 makeup cosmetics market share [55][70], achieving a 3.2% market share by 2022 [70] through superior localization tailored for Vietnamese consumers, directly competing against the 90% foreign-held market [55][70].

**Highly Scalable, Asset-Light Operating Model with Global Quality Standards**
The business model leverages high-quality, outsourced manufacturing in South Korea [14][63] for rapid scalability, supported by an extensive, established nationwide distribution network of approximately 1,000 dedicated agents [14][64].

**Powerful Celebrity-Driven Brand Moat and Digital Engagement**
Brand recognition is underpinned by the creative direction of co-founder Hồ Ngọc Hà [55], a prominent celebrity [44][45] whose high-impact influence accelerates product adoption and builds essential consumer trust in a market prone to counterfeit products [77][76].

**Strategic Product Portfolio Diversification into High-Margin Skincare**
Beyond core color cosmetics (led by the "cash cow" Baby Skin Cushion [55][59]), expansion into the DA by M.O.I skincare sub-brand targets the largest market segment (43% market share [72]) and generated 1.5 million unit sales in one line by the end of 2022 [14].

**Proven Financial Resilience and Significant Revenue Upside**
The company achieved profitability in its early years (FY 2019) [33], providing a strong, profitable foundation to support the current high-growth trajectory implied by the US$12 million revenue context for screening [User Input].
